    About Me

    I am Liang Yancheng, a final-year student of IIIS at Tsinghua University. In Tsinghua, I primarily conducted research under the supervision of Professor Yi Wu. In April 2022, I went to Professor Chuchu Fan‘s lab at MIT as a visiting student.

    Research Interest

    I have a broad interest in machine learning for decision-making, including reinforcement learning, multi-agent systems, game theory, and robotics.

    • Reinforcement learning is a powerful computational base to support decision-making in complex environments. When it is applied to multi-agent systems, it can be used to generate non-trivial behavior patterns. I am interested in RL exploration, multi-agent RL, and RL for game theory.

    • Machine learning and social science like economics, finance, traffic, robotics, and so on.

    • Interdisciplinary research about machine learning and other fields. By bringing knowledge from other fields, machine learning algorithms can be improved. And machine learning models can serve in other domains as computational intelligent agents, like behavior predictors and strategy makers.

    Research Experience

    Low-Switching-Cost Reinforcement Learning

    We design new criteria to reduce the number of policy switches for reinforcement learning.

    AI + Finance: Deep Learning Framework For Risk Prediction

    Risk prediction: Predicting users’ credit risk from their past credit history
    We propose a holistic deep-learning framework for training risk prediction models

    This framework includes important steps for training models such as label selection, data preprocessing, serial and non-serial data modeling, model fusion, loss function selection, and selection methods, providing a framework for applying deep learning to this problem

    Based on this framework, our deep learning model can outperform past methods on real-world data for the first time; the model is already deployed

    Multi-Agent Meta-Exploration by Exploiting State-Action Space

    We show the inefficiency of traditional multi-intelligence exploration methods through some theories

    Based on this theory and observations in previous experiments, we propose a method for training an exploration strategy such that the exploration strategy can exploit the structure of the state action space

    Learning Decentralized Control Barrier Functions to Certificate Multi-agent Reinforcement Learning

    We try to use the Control Barrier Function (CBF) as a tool to learn a safe policy. CBF is a commonly used tool in control theory.

    Unlike conventional control where the CBF is obtained by global sampling, we want to use machine learning methods such as reinforcement learning to perform sampling and make the learned policy more intelligent.

    We also want to provide a distributed CBF framework to accomplish multi-intelligence learning
